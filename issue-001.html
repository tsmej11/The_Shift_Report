§ 06

Know Your Rights

Weingarten Rights · NLRB v. J. Weingarten, Inc. (1975)

You have the right to union representation in any investigatory meeting.

If a manager asks to speak with you and you reasonably believe the conversation could lead to discipline, you have the right to a union delegate present. You must invoke it — the employer does not have to inform you. Memorize this statement:

"If this discussion could in any way lead to my being disciplined or terminated, or affect my personal working conditions, I respectfully request that my union representative be present at this meeting. Until my representative arrives, I choose not to participate in this discussion."

When it applies:

  • You're called into a meeting with HR, your manager, or security
  • You're asked about an incident, medication error, or patient complaint
  • You're asked to give a written statement
  • You reasonably believe discipline could result

When it does NOT apply: A meeting purely to inform you of a decision already made. A routine clinical conversation with no disciplinary purpose. Once you ask for representation, management can choose to end the meeting — that's their right. They cannot retaliate against you for asking.
